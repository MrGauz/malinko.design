Bomb shelter wayfinding system

Concept wayfinding system for bomb shelter in Kyiv

Wayfinding | Interior | Icon system

This project redefines the visual and functional language of bomb shelter network, addressing the urgent need for clarity, cohesion, and target user comfort in high-stress environments. Research revealed a disjointed debatable system: inconsistent signage, absence of a design code, and psychologically taxing conditions amplified by faded colours and non-durable materials. In response, the system was reimagined to deliver both emotional relief and navigational precision.

Key features include:

  • A bright pastel colour palette, selected to reduce stress and shift the atmosphere away from gloom.
  • A colour-coded zoning system, applied across architectural plans and physical spaces to simplify orientation.
  • A custom icon set rooted in the geometry of the Frutiger typeface, balancing legibility with aesthetic cohesion.
  • High-durability materials chosen to resist rust, mould, and delamination, which are common issues in long-unmaintained shelters.

The result is a scalable, psychologically aware design framework that brings structure, safety, and dignity to critical public spaces.
